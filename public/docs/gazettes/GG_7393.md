No. 468

## NOTICE IN TERMS OF REGULATION 19(1) OF THE REGULATIONS REGARDING LICENSING PROCEDURES FOR TELECOMMUNICATIONS AND BROADCASTING SERVICE LICENCES: COMMUNICATIONS ACT, 2009

The Communications Regulatory Authority of Namibia, in terms Regulation 19(1) of the Regulations Regarding Licensing Procedures for Telecommunications and Broadcasting Service Licences, published in Government Gazette No. 4785, General Notice No. 272, dated 29 August 2011 (as amended), hereby gives notice of the following decisions:

## 1. Applications for reconsideration

- (a) The Authority resolved:
- (i) To condone Excellent Communication (Pty) Ltd 's failure to commence with commercial telecommunications services in terms of regulation 17 (4) of the Regulations regarding Licence Conditions;
- (ii) Set aside the Board decision of 21 July 2020, which noted that the Class Comprehensive Telecommunications Service Licence (ECS and ECNS) awarded to Excellent Communication Pty Ltd automatically lapsed on 27 November 2018; and
- (iii) Extend the period within which Excellent Communication Pty Ltd should commence with services with twelve (12) months from date of this Notice.
- (b) The Authority resolved:
- (i) To condone Loc8 Mobile CC 's failure to commence with commercial telecommunications services before 21 December 2019 in terms of regulation 17 (4) of the Regulations regarding Licence Conditions;
- (ii) Set aside the Board decision of 21 July 2020, which noted that the Class Comprehensive Telecommunications Service Licence (ECS and ECNS) awarded to Loc8 Mobile CC automatically lapsed on 21 December 2019; and
- (iii) That Loc8 Mobile CC must provide commercial telecommunications services within twelve (12) months from date of this Notice subject to the following conditions:
- (aa) Submission of quarterly reports on progress of commencement of the MVNO.
- (ab) Submission of the agreement with Telecom Namibia Limited within 30 days from the date of signature.
- (c) The Authority resolved:
- (i) To condone Demshi Investment Holdings (Pty) Ltd 's failure to commence with commercial telecommunications services in terms of regulation 17 (4) of the Regulations regarding Licence Conditions;
- (ii) Set aside the Board decision of 21 July 2020, which noted that the Class Comprehensive Telecommunications Service Licence (ECS and ECNS) awarded to Demshi Investments (Pty) Ltd automatically lapsed on 21 November 2018;

## NOTICE IN TERMS OF REGULATION 4(1) OF THE REGULATIONS PRESCRIBING PROCEDURES REGARDING APPLICATION FOR, AND AMENDMENT, RENEWAL, TRANSFER AND CANCELLATION OF SPECTRUM LICENSES: COMMUNICATIONS ACT, 2009

The Communications Regulatory Authority of Namibia, in terms of regulations 2(1)(c) and 4(1) of the Regulations Prescribing Procedures Regarding Application for, and Amendment, Renewal, Transfer and Cancellation of Spectrum Licenses, published as General Notice No. 104, Government Gazette No. 6888, on 29 April 2019, herewith gives notice that the following radio frequencies under the Frequency Band Plan are available for application for the provision of Broadcasting Services to be conferred on a first-come-first-served basis.

|   Frequencies (MHz) | Power output   | Services to be Provided/ License Category   | Geographical area and transmitter site   |
|---------------------|----------------|---------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
|               100.4 | 500W           | Community Broadcasting                      | Windhoek, Windhoek Höhe                  |
|                89.0 | 500W           | Community Broadcasting                      | Windhoek, Windhoek Höhe                  |
|                95.3 | 500W           | Commercial Broadcasting                     | Windhoek, Windhoek Höhe                  |
|               106.6 | 500W           | Commercial Broadcasting                     | Windhoek, Windhoek Höhe                  |

The Authority will receive applications from the date of this notice until 15 February 2021 at 17H00 . All applications received during that period will be deemed to have been received on 15 February 2021 at 17H00 and will therefore be considered.

All Applicants must note the following:

- a) A Broadcasting Service Licensee that intends to use the radio frequencies listed above must apply to the Authority for a Spectrum License;
- b) Applicants that do not already hold a broadcasting service license must also submit a broadcasting service license application;
- c) Existing Broadcasting Service Licensees must be in good standing with the Authority in that they should not have any outstanding spectrum fees;
- d) Notwithstanding the specific frequency applied for, the Authority will award spectrum licences on competitive basis to the best two applications received for each category;
- e) Spectrum applications must be accompanied by a detailed program schedule, coverage maps, letter of intent in the case of infrastructure sharing or approval from land owner should a new transmitter tower be constructed and a 5 year business case.
- f) All applications received will be considered and awards will be made to the applicant/s that will utilise spectrum resources in such a manner as to ensure the widest possible diversity of programming and the optimal utilisation of spectrum resources.

All applications must be made be send or delivered -

- a) by hand to the head office of CRAN, Communications House, 56 Robert Mugabe Avenue, Windhoek;

- b) by post to CRAN, Private Bag 13309, Windhoek, Namibia;
- c) by electronic mail to CRAN email address: legal@cran.na;
