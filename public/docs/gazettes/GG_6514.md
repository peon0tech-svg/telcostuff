No. 18

## NOTICE OF WITHDRAWAL OF REGULATIONS PRESCRIBING LIMITS OF TARIFFS FOR TELECOMMUNICATIONS SERVICES ('PRICE CAP REGULATIONS')

The Communications Regulatory Authority of Namibia herewith gives notice in terms of regulation 11(b) of the Regulations Regarding Rule-making Procedures as published in the Government Gazette No. 4630 General Notice No. 334 dated 17 December 2010 that it has withdrawn the Notice of intention to make Regulations Prescribing Limits of Tariffs for Telecommunications Services ('Price Cap Regulations') as published in the Government Gazette No. 6322 General Notice No. 156 dated 1 June 2017.

The reason for withdrawal of the said Notice is that the Authority is currently reviewing and updating the current cost study on these Regulations.

Once the study has been updated, the Authority will initiate new rule making proceedings and will issue a new notice of intention to make regulations in future.

## NOTICE IN TERMS OF REGULATION 19(1) OF THE REGULATIONS REGARDING LICENSING PROCEDURES FOR TELECOMMUNICATIONS AND BROADCASTING SERVICE LICENCES AND SPECTRUM USE LICENCES

The Communications Regulatory Authority of Namibia, in terms Regulation 19(1) of the Regulations Regarding Licensing Procedures for Telecommunications and Broadcasting Service Licences and Spectrum Use Licences, in Government Gazette No. 4785, General Notice No. 272, dated 29 August 2011 (as amended), herewith gives notice of the following decisions (which decisions are effective from date of this notice):

## 1. Award of Spectrum Use Licenses

## 1.1 MWireless (Pty) Ltd

The Authority resolved to award spectrum use licences to MWireless (Pty) Ltd for the provision of fixed satellite services in the Republic of Namibia or any part thereof as indicated below:

- a) 14.025 GHz uplink (36 MHz bandwidth); and
- b) 10.975 GHz downlink (36 MHz bandwidth).

## 1.2 Radiance Consulting and Trading Services CC t/a Rapids FM

The Authority resolved to approve the award of a spectrum use licence to Radiance Consulting and Training Services CC t/a Rapids FM for implementation of a studio link as indicated below:

- a) 460.0500 MHz with a maximum output power of 15W.

## 1.3 MTN Business Solutions Namibia

The Authority resolved to award spectrum use licences to MTN Business Solutions Namibia (Pty) Ltd for the provision for telecommunications services in the Republic of Namibia or any part thereof as indicated below:

- a) 3538.5-3558.5 MHz, and
- b) 1940-1960 MHz (uplink) paired 2130-2150 MHz (downlink).

## 2. Award of Class Comprehensive Telecommunications Service Licence (ECS and ECNS)

## 2.1 Radio Electronic CC

The Authority resolved to award of a Class Comprehensive Telecommunications Service Licence (ECS and ECNS) to Radio Electronic CC.

## 2.2 Schoemans Technology (Pty) Ltd

The Authority resolved to award a Class Comprehensive Telecommunications Service Licence (ECS and ECNS) to Schoemans Technologies (Pty) Ltd for the provision for telecommunications services in the Republic of Namibia or any part thereof.

The Authority further resolved to decline the application for Spectrum Use Licence by Schoemans Technologies (Pty) Ltd as frequencies applied for are in a spectrum use license exempt band.

## 2.3 Focus Engineering Services CC

The Authority resolved to approve the award of a Class Comprehensive Telecommunications Service Licence (ECS and ECNS) to Focus Engineering Services CC.

The Authority resolved to decline the award of a Spectrum Use Licence to Focus Engineering CC, as the frequencies applied for are in a spectrum use license exempt band.

## 3. Application to extend period of commencement of utilisation of the Spectrum Use Licence

## 3.1 MTN Business Solutions Namibia (Pty) Ltd

The Authority resolved to grant an extension to MTN Business Solutions Namibia (Pty) Ltd to commence utilisation of its spectrum licence for 2387-2397 MHz on or before 31 March 2018.

## 3.2 Namibia TV &amp; DVD Productions CC

The Authority resolved to grant Namibia TV &amp; DVD Productions CC an extension to commence broadcasting services within ten (10) months from date of this notice.

## 4. Application for amendment of Spectrum Use Licence

## 4.1 Telecom Namibia Limited

The Authority resolved to approve the amendment of the Spectrum Use Licence awarded to Telecom Namibia Limited for 2540.000-2592.000 MHz and 2656.000-2690.000 MHz to allow for the provision of fixed and mobile services to facilitate the implementation of a TDD-LTE network to provide broadband services to its customers within the borders of the Republic of Namibia.

## 5. Application for renewal of Community Broadcasting Service Licence

## 5.1 Oranjemund Community Radio

The Authority resolved to approve the renewal of the Community Broadcasting Service Licence awarded to Oranjemund Community Radio on 12 December 2012, for a further period of five (5) years commencing from the date of this notice.

## 6. Applications for a Community Broadcasting Service Licence and Spectrum Use Licence

## 6.1 Adventist Development and Relief Agency Namibia

The Authority resolved to award a community broadcasting service Licence to Adventist Development and Relief Agency Namibia for providing broadcasting services in Gobabis, Otjiwarongo, Rundu and Keetmanshoop.

The Authority further resolved to award Spectrum use licences to Adventist Development and Relief Agency Namibia as indicated below:

- a) Gobabis (89.2 MHz - 500 W);
- b) Otjiwarongo (103.4 MHz - 500 W);
- c) Rundu (104.3 MHz - 500 W); and
- d) Keetmanshoop (92.9 MHz - 500 W).

## 7. Application for reconsideration in respect of a Spectrum Use Licence

## 7.1 Converged Telecommunications Solutions (Pty) Ltd

The Authority resolved to decline the application for reconsideration submitted by Converged Telecommunications Solutions (Pty) Ltd on 27 August 2017.

No. 20
