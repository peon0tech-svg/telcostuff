## NOTICEINTERMSOFTHEREGULATIONSREGARDINGLICENSINGPROCEDURES FORTELECOMMUNICATIONSANDBROADCASTINGSERVICELICENCESAND SPECTRUMUSELICENCES

The CommunicationsRegulatoryAuthority ofNamibia,in terms of sections101readwithregulations 6and 1l of theRegulations Regarding LicensingProcedures for Telecommunications and BroadcastingServiceLicencesandSpectrumUseLicences",publishedinGovernment GazetteNo. 4785,NoticeNo.272,dated29August2011(as amended),herewithgivesnotice that the application foraspectrumuselicencesubmittedbyMobileTelecommunicationsLimitedhasbeendeniedin whole.Thefollowing arethereasonsfor thedecision:

1. MobileTelecommunicationsLimitedsubmittedanapplicationforaspectrumuselicense fortelecommunicationsservicestotheAuthorityon17June2011forconsideration.Due process inrespect of the applicationwasfollowed in terms of the procedures outlined in theRegulationsregardingLicensingProceduresforTelecommunications andBroadcasting ServiceLicencesandSpectrumUseLicences.

3. MobileTelecommunications Limitedwasissuedwith aClass Comprehensive TelecommunicationsServiceLicense(ECS&amp;ECNS)by theAuthorityin termsofsection 38of theActandtheRegulationsregardingTransitionalProceduresforTelecommunications andBroadcastingServiceLicences andSpectrumUseLicences aspublished in Government GazeteNo.4946,General NoticeNo.121 dated 20March 2012.
4. When considering the award of a spectrum use licence,the Authority must consider the availability ofspectrum as appliedfor.
5. In compliance with Section 101 of the Act and theRegulations regarding Licensing Procedures,the applicationwasfor2x20MHzFDDinthe 2.6 GHzspectrumbandfor the implementation of LTE services.Following due processin terms ofregulation11 of the Regulations regardingLicensing Procedures,the Authority published a notice in respect oftheapplicationinGovernmentGazetteNo.4756,GeneralNoticeNo217,dated15July 2011.The publication allowed for fourteen (14) days for public comments from the date of publicationof theNoticeinthe Gazette.Nopubliccommentswerereceivedandsubsequently noreplycommentswererequiredfromMobileTelecommunicationsLimited.

6. Notall technicaldocumentsthatarerequiredbysection Gofthespectrumuselicense applicationformwassubmittedwith the applicationform.TheAuthority could therefore, notproceedwiththeevaluationoftheapplication.TheAuthorityformallyrequestedthe outstandingtechnicalinformationfromMobileTelecommunicationsLimitedinterms ofregulation6(4)oftheRegulationsregardingLicensingProcedures on8March2014. MobileTelecommunicationsLimitedsubmittedalloutstandingtechnicalinformationto theAuthorityon29April2014andthuscompliedwithallrequirementssetforthinthe applicationformfora spectrum use licence.Consequently theAuthority proceededwith the consideration and analysis of the application.
7. TheAuthorityhasnotissued anynewspectrumuselicencesinthe2.6GHzspectrumband sinceitsestablishmenton18May201l.Allspectrumuselicensesheldbylicenseesin thisbandhasoriginallybeenissuedbytheNamibianCommunicationsCommissionand subsequentlytransitionedbytheAuthorityintermsofSection135of theActandRegulations regardingTransitionalProceduresforTelecommunicationsandBroadcastingService LicencesandSpectrumUseLicences.Thetablebelow depicts the current assignment of spectrumuselicences in the2.6GHzspectrumband asfrom13September2012,whichis the date of completion of the transitioning process for spectrum use licences.Please note: Thecolumnheadingsindicate thevariousspectrumuselicenceholderswhilst thecoloured blocksindicatethespectrumassignmentsforeachlicenceholder.
